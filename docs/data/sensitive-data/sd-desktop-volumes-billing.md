# Retroactive billing: how to request Billing Units for SD Desktop external volumes 

## Intorduction

Starting from 26 May 2026, SD Desktop external volumes will begin consuming Cloud-type billing units, which will be automatically allocated to your CSC project instead of manually assigned. Volumes usage from January 2026 to 26 May 2026 will also be charged retroactively.

If by then your project does not have enough billing units, the CSC project Billing Units balance will go negative, your virtual desktop will be paused, the project will be closed after 60 days, and all data will be permanently deleted 90 days after closure. To avoid disruption, please apply for additional billing units via the MyCSC portal as soon as possible, as processing times may vary.

Dear SD Desktop users,
We would like to inform you of an important upcoming change to SD Desktop billing unit consumption that requires advance planning to avoid disruption.
Starting from 26 May 2026, SD Desktop storage volumes will consume Cloud-type billing units. Previously, billing units were assigned manually to your organization. With this change, billing unit consumption will be automatically assigned to your CSC project.
In addition, on 26 May 2026, billing units consumed by your virtual desktop volumes will be reallocated retroactively for the period from January 2026 to 26 May 2026.
If your CSC project does not have sufficient billing units at that time:


        
      The balance will go negative

        
      Your virtual desktop will be paused

        
      The project will be closed after 60 days

        
      All related data will be permanently deleted 90 days after closure

Required actions

To prepare for this change, please take the following steps:


        
      Review how many external storage volumes are used in your virtual desktop

        
      Apply for additional billing units via the My CSC portal (my.csc.fi)

Please note that processing times vary depending on the package, so we recommend taking action as soon as possible



        
      You can find step-by-step guidance and common questions in the documentation
